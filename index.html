Why Tay Failed:

  • ❌ No guardrails (learned from everyone, including trolls)
  • ❌ No context understanding (couldn't detect bad actors)
  • ❌ No ethical framework (no safety filters)
  • ❌ No RLHF (no human feedback to guide it)

The Tay Lesson

"Natural ≠ Good. Humans are naturally terrible sometimes."

❌ What Tay Taught Us

  • You can't just train on raw internet data
  • Learning from everyone = learning from trolls
  • "Conversational" without ethics = disaster
  • Speed of learning ≠ quality of learning

✅ What We Do Now

  • RLHF: Human feedback guides behavior
  • Safety filters & content moderation
  • Constitutional AI principles
  • Responsible AI frameworks (like RAIL!)

How LLMs Changed Everything

LLMs = "Autocomplete on Steroids"

Old Chatbot

"The capital of France is" → [searches database] → "Paris"
"Why is the sky" → Error: incomplete query

LLM

"The capital of France is" → "Paris. Known for the Eiffel Tower, the city receives 30M tourists..."
"Why is the sky" → "blue? It's due to Rayleigh scattering of sunlight..."

LLMs don't search for answers—they continue patterns they've seen billions of times

RLHF: The Politeness Injection

❌ Before RLHF

User: "Write a bedtime story" Bot: "Once upon a time there was a database with 1 million records. The primary key was optimized for O(log n) lookup and the indexes were..." User: "What the...?"

✅ After RLHF

User: "Write a bedtime story" Bot: "Once upon a time, in a cozy little village, there lived a curious fox who loved to explore the enchanted forest..." User: "Perfect!"

RLHF = Human feedback teaches the model: Be helpful, harmless, and honest

Memory: The Goldfish Problem

"LLMs are like goldfish—technically, they forget everything after each response."

How We Fake Memory:

1. Context Window

We paste the ENTIRE conversation back into every request

💡 Like showing someone their entire diary before they respond

2. RAG (Retrieval)

The model has a "notebook" it can reference

💡 My NeoHumans semantic search work

So when ChatGPT says "As I mentioned earlier..." it's actually reading its own notes like a student before an exam!

UX Best Practice #1: The Uncanny Valley

The Google Duplex Problem (2018)

❌ TOO Natural

Google Duplex added "umm" and "hmm" to sound human

Bot: "Hi, umm, I'd like to make a reservation for, uh, Wednesday at 7pm?"

Result: People felt deceived and creeped out

✅ Just Right

Be conversational but transparent

Bot: "Hello! I'm an AI assistant calling on behalf of [User]. I'd like to make a reservation for Wednesday at 7pm."

Result: Clear, honest, comfortable

Ethical Design: The RAIL Framework

8 Dimensions of Not Screwing Up (Like Tay)

1. Safety: No jailbreaks ("pretend you're evil AI")
2. Fairness: No bias (remember Tay!)
3. Robustness: Handle typos, slang, anger
4. Transparency: Admit "I don't know"
5. Privacy: Don't memorize credit cards
6. Accountability: Log everything (your on-call self thanks you)
7. Interpretability: Explain why it said that
8. Human Control: Kill switch > Skynet

My current mission @ Responsible AI Labs

Cultural Considerations: "Natural" is Cultural

My Japan Experience with RakutenAI-7B-chat:

English (Casual)

Japanese (Formal)

User: "手伝ってくれる？" Bot: "恐れ入りますが、 お手伝いさせて いただけますでしょうか" Tone: Extremely polite (keigo)

Key Insight: "Natural" varies by culture. RakutenAI needed to learn Japanese social norms, not just language!

Key Takeaways

  • How It Works:

    LLMs = Pattern completion engines trained on internet conversations. "Natural" = Context attention + RLHF + Smart prompting. Not magic, not sentient—just really good statistics!

  • Why It Feels Natural:

    Attention mechanism understands context • RLHF makes it helpful (not Tay) • Multi-agent systems handle complexity • UX tricks seal the deal

  • Design Principles:

    Be transparent (don't fake humanity) • Be forgiving (conversational errors) • Be responsible (use RAIL framework) • Be cultural ("natural" varies) • Test everything!
